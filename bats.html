Allocated Bourbon Transparency

Promoting real-time visibility of allocations and hard-to-find bourbons across participating ABC boards.

ABC boards retain complete control of their inventory systems. Participating boards decide how to supply the allocations scan data from existing store-level workflows. We handle the processing, publishing, and permanent archive for public transparency.

The problem boards are already managing

Allocated bourbon creates recurring pressure on store teams, public websites, and public confidence.

Repeated inventory searches

High-demand products can drive frequent customer searches and automated traffic against public inventory tools.

Store phone calls

Staff time is consumed by repeated questions about whether allocated bottles have arrived or remain available.

Fairness concerns

When allocation visibility is inconsistent, customers may assume insiders or small groups have an advantage.

The solution: publish allocation activity from store scan data

Participating stores supply scan data from their existing receiving workflow. Bourbon Pourcast handles processing, public visibility, archive, and consumer traffic.

Store scan occurs

Allocated bottles continue to be scanned by store staff through the store's existing receiving or inventory workflow.

Scan data is supplied

Store-level scan data is shared by feed, export, API, secure file drop, or other low-friction method approved by the board.

We process and publish

Bourbon Pourcast identifies allocation activity, formats the public record, and publishes it in near real time.

Archive is maintained

Each published allocation record becomes part of a permanent searchable archive maintained by Bourbon Pourcast.

Board-facing value proposition

The platform is designed to reduce friction for boards while increasing public confidence.

No inventory system replacement

Boards keep their current POS, receiving, inventory, and store operations while Bourbon Pourcast handles the public-facing transparency layer.

Reduced inquiry burden

Customers can self-serve allocation information instead of calling stores or repeatedly searching board systems.

Public confidence

Timestamped visibility helps demonstrate that allocation activity is published fairly, consistently, and without preferential access.

Pilot program concept

A limited pilot can validate the workflow, measure consumer engagement, and demonstrate reduced pressure on stores and public inventory tools.

  • One participating board and selected stores
  • Limited allocated product list
  • No replacement of existing inventory systems
  • Public feed plus permanent historical archive
  • Board feedback before broader rollout
Participation options

Store scan feed, periodic export, API, CSV, secure file drop, or other board-approved data handoff.
